A Lava Flow Simulation of Pu'u 'O'o

Using a very simple lava flow model designed by L. Connor and C. Connor, I erupted lava out of Pu'u 'O'o Volcano, the currently erupting vent within the East Rift of Kilauea, Hawaii.

The model relies on topography and simplifies (often poorly quantified) geophysical parameters by replacing them with a "residual thickness." Once this thickness is overcome in any location, it will pass lava on to neighboring locations downhill. Whether this is an oversimplification or not, it makes a remarkably realistic flow!
